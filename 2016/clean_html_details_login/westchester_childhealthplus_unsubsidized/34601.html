Other Services
Benefit Description
Autism spectrum disorder screening, diagnosis and treatment Help tooltip
Services that are prescribed or ordered by a licensed Physician or a licensed psychologist and are determined to be medically necessary for the screening, diagnosis, and treatment of autism spectrum disorder. $45,000 maximum benefit per calendar year.
Bariatric Surgery Help tooltip
Weight loss surgery.
Breast reconstructive surgery following mastectomy, lumpectomy or lymph node dissection Help tooltip
Breast reconstructive surgery following mastectomy, lumpectomy or lymph node dissection.
Chemotherapy Help tooltip
Chemotherapy in an outpatient facility or in a Health Care Professional's office. Orally-administered anti-cancer drugs are covered under the prescription drug benefit.
Diabetic equipment, supplies, education and self-management Help tooltip
Diabetic equipment, supplies, and self-management education if recommended or prescribed by a physician or other licensed health care professional.
Dialysis Help tooltip
Dialysis treatments of an acute or chronic kidney ailment.
Mastectomy Care Help tooltip
Services for members undergoing a lymph node dissection, lumpectomy, mastectomy or partial mastectomy for the treatment of breast cancer and any physical complications arising from the mastectomy, including lymphedema.
Medical/Surgical Second Opinion Help tooltip
A second medical opinion by an appropriate specialist or surgeon.
Oral Surgery Help tooltip
Limited dental and oral surgical procedures related to accident or injury, congenital disease or anomaly, functional impairment, removal of tumors or cysts, and surgical/nonsurgical medical procedures for temporomandibular joint disorders and orthognathic surgery.
Reconstructive and corrective surgery Help tooltip
Reconstructive and corrective surgery when performed to correct a congenital birth defect resulting in a functional defect or when it is incidental to surgery or follows surgery that was necessitated by trauma, infection or disease of the involved part; or is otherwise medically necessary.
Transplants Help tooltip
Transplants determined to be nonexperimental and non-investigational. Covered transplants include but are not limited to: kidney, corneal, liver, heart, and heart/lung transplants; and bone marrow transplants for aplastic anemia, leukemia, severe combined immunodeficiency disease and Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome.
Pediatric Dental
Benefit Description
Basic Dental Care Help tooltip
Routine dental examinations, visits and services for children performed in a dentist's office covered once within 6 month consecutive period.
Emergency Dental Care Help tooltip
Dental services considered emergency in nature.
Major Dental Care Help tooltip
Services, including procedures for treatment of diseased pulp chambers and pulp canals, where Hospitalization is not required, removable complete or partial dentures, including six (6) months follow-up care; and additional services include insertion of identification slips, repairs, relines and rebases and treatment of cleft palate.
Orthodontia Help tooltip
Coverage for medically necessary orthodontia.
Preventive Dental Care Help tooltip
1 per 6 month period. Dental examination, consultation covered once within 6 month consecutive period. Sealants, x-rays, etc covered according to standard periodicity/patient age.
Pediatric Vision
Benefit Description
Contact Lenses/Frames Help tooltip
1 per year. Coverage provided once in any 12 month period. Additional frames may be provided with appropriate documentation.
Prescription Lenses Help tooltip
1 per year. Coverage provided once in any 12 month period. Additional lenses may be provided with appropriate documentation.
Routine Vision Exams Help tooltip
Emergency, preventive and routine vision care.
Hospitalization
Benefit Description
Delivery and all Inpatient Services for Maternity Care Help tooltip
Delivery and inpatient maternity care in a hospital for the mother, and inpatient newborn care in a hospital for the infant, for at least 48 hours following a normal delivery and at least 96 hours following a caesarean section delivery.
Inpatient Hospital Services Help tooltip
Health care you get when you're admitted as a patient to a health care facility, like a hospital or skilled nursing facility.
Inpatient Physician and Surgical Services Help tooltip
Services performed by a physican or a surgeon in an inpatient setting.
Laboratory and Imaging Services
Benefit Description
Diagnostic Test (X-Ray and Lab Work) Help tooltip
X-rays and lab work that doctors use in the diagnosis of a medical condition.
Imaging (CT/PET Scans, MRI) Help tooltip
Imaging technologies such as CT and PET Scans and MRIs that doctors use in the treatment of a medical condition.
Rehabilitative and Habilitative Services and Devices
Benefit Description
Durable Medical Equipment Help tooltip
Orthotic Exclusion: devices prescribed solely for use during sports
Habilitative Services Help tooltip
Health care services that help you keep, learn, or improve skills and functioning for daily living. Examples include therapy for a child who isn't walking or talking at the expected age. These services may include physical and occupational therapy, speech-language pathology, and other services for people with disabilities in a variety of inpatient and/or outpatient settings.
Hearing Aids Help tooltip
Electronic amplifying devices designed to bring sound more effectively into the ear consisting of a microphone, amplifier and receiver.
Inpatient Rehabilitative Services Help tooltip
Health care services that help a person keep, get back, or improve skills and functioning for daily living that have been lost or impaired because a person was sick, hurt, or disabled. These services consist of physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy in an inpatient setting.
Outpatient Rehabilitative Services Help tooltip
Short Term Coverage for Occupational, Physical and Therapies; Coverage for Speech Therapy up to 2 months, starting from the first day of therapy.
Prosthetic Devices Help tooltip
Prosthetic Exclusions: Cranial Prosthesis (i.e. wigs); dental prosthesis unless resulting from medical injury/illness.
Emergency Services
Benefit Description
Emergency Room Services Help tooltip
Healthcare services you get in an emergency room.
Emergency Transportation/Ambulance Help tooltip
Ambulance services for an emergency medical condition. Exclusion: Air Ambulance
Urgent Care Centers or Facilities Help tooltip
A licensed facility (except Hospitals) that provides care for an illness, injury or condition serious enough to require care right away, but not so severe as to require emergency room care.

Benefit Description
Home Health Services Help tooltip
Health care services a person receives at home.
Hospice Services Help tooltip
Services to provide comfort and support for persons in the last stages of a terminal illness and their families.
Other Practitioner Office Visit (Nurse, Physican Assistant) Help tooltip
Visit to a clinician for health services that cover a range of prevention, wellness, and treatment for common illnesses.
Outpatient Facility Fee Help tooltip
Amount charged to pay for the use of an outpatient facility.
Outpatient Surgery Physician/Surgical Services Help tooltip
Amount charged to pay for the services of the clinican performing healthcare or surgical services in an outpatient setting.
PCP Office Visits (Injury or Illness) Help tooltip
Visit to a primary care provider for health services that cover a range of prevention, wellness, and treatment for common illnesses.
Specialist Visit Help tooltip
Visits to a physician to diagnose, manage, prevent or treat certain types of symptoms and conditions related to a specific disease or condition.

Benefit Description
Mental/Behavioral Health Inpatient Services Help tooltip
Diagnosis and treatment of mental, nervous and emotional disorders performed in an inpatient setting.
Mental/Behavioral Health Outpatient Services Help tooltip
Diagnosis and treatment of mental, nervous and emotional disorders performed in an outpatient setting.

Diagnosis and treatment of alcoholism and/or substance use and/or dependency in an inpatient setting. This includes coverage for detoxification and/or rehabilitation services as a consequence of chemical use and/or substance use.

Benefit Description
